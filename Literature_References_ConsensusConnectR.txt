CORE NETWORK NEUROSCIENCE REFERENCES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Rubinov, M., & Sporns, O. (2010). Complex network measures of brain
   connectivity: Uses and interpretations. NeuroImage, 52(3), 1059-1069.
   https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuroimage.2009.10.003

   * ESSENTIAL CITATION - Brain Connectivity Toolbox paper
   * Defines standard network metrics used in connectivity analysis
   * Over 10,000 citations - foundational paper for the field
   * Cite for: degree, betweenness, clustering coefficient, modularity definitions

2. Bullmore, E., & Sporns, O. (2009). Complex brain networks: Graph theoretical
   analysis of structural and functional systems. Nature Reviews Neuroscience,
   10(3), 186-198.
   https://doi.org/10.1038/nrn2575

   * ESSENTIAL CITATION - Seminal review of brain network analysis
   * Establishes graph theory framework for neuroscience
   * Cite for: introduction to network neuroscience concepts

3. Sporns, O. (2011). Networks of the Brain. MIT Press.

   * Comprehensive textbook on brain networks
   * Cite for: theoretical background on network organization

4. Bassett, D. S., & Sporns, O. (2017). Network neuroscience. Nature
   Neuroscience, 20(3), 353-364.
   https://doi.org/10.1038/nn.4502

   * Modern overview of network neuroscience field
   * Cite for: current state of the field in introduction

SHRINKAGE CORRELATION:

5. Schäfer, J., & Strimmer, K. (2005). A shrinkage approach to large-scale
   covariance matrix estimation and implications for functional genomics.
   Statistical Applications in Genetics and Molecular Biology, 4(1), Article 32.
   https://doi.org/10.2202/1544-6115.1175

   * ESSENTIAL CITATION - Shrinkage correlation method
   * Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage estimator
